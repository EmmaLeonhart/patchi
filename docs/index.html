PATCHi

A live research project that is formalizing — and building a working reference implementation of — Pygmalion's theory of cognition: the idea that meaning is made of relations between words, that words are agreements on labels, that context is the base relation everything else is built on, and that memory, logic, and thought can be assembled from these pieces into a real machine.

Findings

First measured result is in (2026-06-25) — Pygmalion's similarity-weighted blending operator, tested against a plain additive average and raw vectors on a controlled synthetic denoising task (full write-up: FINDINGS.md). The result is real and nuanced, not a victory lap:

  • Reconstruction denoises strongly over raw vectors when the neighbourhood carries signal — up to +0.14 Spearman over raw.
  • The similarity weighting earns its keep mainly when neighbourhoods are trustworthy (low noise, sharp weighting): +0.10 to +0.14 over a flat additive average — a repeatable margin, locked in by a regression test.
  • The advantage decays with noise, and over-sharpening can backfire (slightly negative under heavy noise). At a middling setting it barely beats additive (+0.0035): the operator is a trustworthy-neighbourhood denoiser, not a free win.

Scope, stated plainly: synthetic data, not real embeddings. The real-embeddings run (GloVe + WordSim-353) is the queued next step — it needs downloads unavailable in the offline build, so it is named as a blocker, not faked.
